Self-Balancing Cube

Not Completed

The Beginning

Although this project remains a work in progress, significant strides have already been taken. The inspiration for our endeavor came from the remarkable Cubliâ€”a metal cube demonstrating exceptional balancing abilities on its edge, corner, and even the capability to leap onto its corner. Intrigued by this concept, we opted to create our version of the Cubli, opting to utilize 3D printed materials instead. Our preliminary research involved studying various videos showcasing others' attempts at building similar devices, as well as delving into the insightful demonstrations shared by Cubli's creators. Additionally, we delved into the fundamental principles of how reaction wheels function to enhance our understanding. With a comprehensive understanding of the technology and the design insights gleaned from our research, we enthusiastically embarked on the journey by initiating the process of designing and procuring the required components.

The Middle

The initial phase of the design process revolved around creating the reaction wheels. My primary objective was to optimize their performance by striking a balance between minimizing weight at the center while increasing weight at the edges to achieve enhanced torque. To achieve this, I strategically removed material from the center of the wheels and incorporated holes along the edges, where I would later attach screws and nuts. For the outer walls of the cube, I kept the design straightforward, ensuring that visibility into the cube was unobstructed. Additionally, I designed a simple yet effective hinge mechanism to facilitate preliminary testing. With the design taking shape, the next step involved acquiring the essential components required for the project. I meticulously selected high-quality motors, a reliable MPU 1060 (combining accelerometer and gyroscope capabilities), a suitable battery, and a capable microcontroller. Having assembled all the necessary components, the stage was set for the crucial testing phase, where the true potential of the Cubli-inspired creation would be unveiled.

The End

The testing phase has been a pivotal yet regrettably halted stage in the development process. Implementing the hinge system, I commenced testing the code to achieve the delicate balancing act on a single corner in one axis. The progress was incredibly promising, and the device was steadily approaching the desired equilibrium point. However, just as we were on the cusp of a breakthrough, the start of the school year abruptly disrupted our momentum. Despite this temporary setback, the passion and commitment to this project remain unwavering. My determination to uncover the root cause of the oscillations that caused the device to lose balance has only grown stronger. I am eagerly anticipating the opportunity to pick up where we left off and resume refining the code and addressing any underlying issues.

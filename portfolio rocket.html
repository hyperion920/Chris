Hybrid-Propellant Rocket

Completed

The Beginning

Creating the rocket involved an extensive array of calculations, ranging from determining the mass flow rate of nitrous to conducting plume simulations. These meticulous calculations were essential in ensuring the rocket's successful launch. However, despite our efforts, we encountered an unexpected hurdle further along the development process: we struggled to achieve the desired thrust we had initially envisioned. Nevertheless, undeterred, we meticulously calculated every aspect of the rocket, which served as the foundation for our subsequent design phase. My role specifically revolved around the combustion chamber team, wherein we dedicated our efforts to developing crucial components such as the injector (including the body, manifold, and coin), nozzle, fuel grain, retainer, and the chamber itself. Each team collaborated seamlessly, ensuring that every part seamlessly fit within the CAD model during the assembly phase. With the design meticulously mapped out, it was finally time to move forward and commence the manufacturing process.

The Middle

The manufacturing phase proved to be a lengthy endeavor, further complicated by the looming time constraints posed by the end of the school term. With the urgency to complete the rocket and conduct several static fires before the competition, we found ourselves racing against the clock. Given my responsibilities within the combustion chamber team, I took charge of machining critical components such as the retainer and injector body, while also providing assistance with the nozzle and chamber. Machining the nozzle was particularly nerve-racking due to the fragile nature of graphite, which required careful handling to avoid breakage. Additionally, the stock material utilized for the chamber exhibited significant runout, posing additional challenges. Despite encountering numerous setbacks, we persevered and managed to successfully fabricate all the necessary pieces within the available timeframe and all the required parts coming out with perfect tolerances and finishes. Every part coming together and forming the modelled rocket in real life.This allowed us to proceed with testing, both for the purpose of identifying potential improvements and collecting vital data required for competition submission. Nevertheless, the testing phase itself presented its own set of obstacles, demanding our commitment to resolving them effectively.

The End

The anticipation was palpable as we prepared for our first static fire test, with team morale soaring high and everyone eager to witness the mesmerizing plume and Mach diamonds in action. Regrettably, unforeseen issues with our ignitors and fluid system leaks thwarted our plans, forcing us to settle for a cold flow test instead. Despite this setback, the cold flow test yielded valuable data that guided our subsequent improvements. After dedicating a week to resolving the identified problems, we embarked on another test, which proved to be a resounding success. It was an exhilarating moment, etching itself as one of the most thrilling experiences of my life. However, upon reviewing the data, we realized our thrust fell significantly short of the targeted 800-pound force. Suspecting that the issue lay in the mass flow rate, we diligently recalculated the required figures over the next few days. Armed with the recalibrated calculations, we remachined the critical components, ensuring they aligned with our revised approach. Another round of testing followed, and although the data showed improvement, it still fell short of our expectations. Time became a pressing constraint, leading us to make a series of desperate attempts to refine our rocket's performance. However, before we could achieve the desired outcome, the clock ran out. With a mix of anticipation and fear, we proceeded to the competition, fervently hoping that our rocket would soar to success. And, to our immense satisfaction, it did. The moment of liftoff justified all the painstaking effort we had invested throughout the year. We will leverage the wealth of data gathered from this launch to enhance the design and performance of next year's rocket, determined to push the boundaries even further.

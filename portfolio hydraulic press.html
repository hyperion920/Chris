Hydraulic Press

Not Completed

The Beginning

My journey into creating a hydraulic press began when I stumbled upon a captivating YouTube video showcasing the construction process using PVC pipes. Intrigued by the concept, I decided to embark on my own version, drawing inspiration from the video. However, one crucial difference set my approach apart—I intended to leverage 3D printing technology, believing it would streamline the process by allowing me to design and print the components instead of manually crafting them. Regrettably, I made an early misstep by neglecting to thoroughly research the properties of the material I had chosen, namely PLA. Had I taken the time to familiarize myself with its key features and limitations, I could have charted a more informed course. Nevertheless, undeterred by this oversight, I immersed myself in the CAD software, meticulously designing every piece required for the hydraulic press—from the cylinder to the piston and the stand—ensuring that they seamlessly converged into a cohesive whole. And thus, with the designs finalized, it was time to set the wheels in motion and proceed with the printing process.

The Middle

As I delved into the realm of 3D printing, an arduous reality quickly dawned on me—it was nothing short of a nightmare. Achieving the perfect tolerances for my printed pieces to fit together flawlessly, particularly the piston and cylinder, proved to be an excruciating process, riddled with trial and error. Weeks and months elapsed as I toiled away, continually printing and reprinting, striving to attain a functional hydraulic cylinder complete with installed O-rings and other crucial components. However, when I subjected it to a water test, disappointment flooded in as the water leaked through every crevice. It became evident that PLA, the material I had utilized, possessed a porous nature, leading to frustrating leaks through the plastic itself. I felt defeated, but the relentless grip of determination compelled me to salvage the project that had consumed so much of my time and effort. It was at that moment that I resolved to reconstruct the hydraulic press using a more robust material—metal.

The End

Eager to forge ahead, I dedicated the following week to mastering the skills required for operating a CNC and manual lathe. Armed with my meticulously designed CAD models, the only missing piece of the puzzle was generating the necessary CAM files. After familiarizing myself with the corresponding software, I swiftly generated the CAM files for all the parts, marking the commencement of the machining process. To my surprise, manufacturing the components using the lathe proved to be a significantly smoother endeavor compared to 3D printing. Not only was it easier, but the experience of working with the lathe was undeniably enjoyable, evoking a thrilling sense of achievement. I successfully machined nearly all the required pieces, only to encounter a minor setback when I realized that two of my designed parts had holes that were too small to accommodate the angle fittings. As the start of classes approached, I had to temporarily halt the project, but this year I am determined to finish the project, increasing the size of the system by adding multiple sets of valves for safety and accessibility.
